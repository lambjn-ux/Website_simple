Mr. Josh Lamb is a fellowship trained hip and knee replacement specialist working in the world-famous Wrightington Hospital and the University of Bristol.

Josh completed Trauma and Orthopaedic surgery specialist training in Yorkshire and has completed hip and knee arthroplasty fellowships in Waikato (NZ) and Wrightington (UK). This included subspecialist training in primary hip and knee surgery, including robotic and navigated surgery. Fellowship training also included advanced techniques in hip and knee revision surgery. His current clinical practice includes primary hip and knee replacement and revision surgery.

He is an expert in the association of implant design and risk of post-operative complications. His academic work helps to inform implant selection for his patients to maximise the chances of a positive outcome after surgery. Mr Lamb is an invited speaker nationally and internationally on the subject and continues his work with the University of Bristol.

Mr Lamb's philosophy is centred on patient well-being. He understands that joint pain can significantly impact your life, and he is dedicated to offering personalised treatment plans, clear communication, and a supportive environment from your initial consultation through recovery. He believes in empowering his patients with knowledge and ensuring they feel safe and cared for every step of the way. Your journey to restored mobility and a pain-free life is his priority.
